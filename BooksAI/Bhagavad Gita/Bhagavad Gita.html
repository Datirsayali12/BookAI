Bhagavad Gita

Chapter 1: Arjuna Vishada Yoga

Sanskrit Shloka

The Bhagavad Gita begins with the Sanskrit shloka:

"धृतराष्ट्र उवाच |
धर्मक्षेत्रे कुरुक्षेत्रे समवेता युयुत्सवः |
मामकाः पाण्डवाश्चैव किमकुर्वत सञ्जय ||1||"

Meaning

The English translation of the above shloka is:

"Dhritarashtra said: O Sanjay, assembled in the holy land of Kurukshetra and desiring to fight, what did my sons and the sons of Pandu do?"

This sets the stage for the dialogue between Arjuna and Lord Krishna, highlighting the battlefield setting and the impending conflict.

Chapter 2: Sankhya Yoga

Sanskrit Shloka

The Bhagavad Gita verse in its original Sanskrit:

क्लैब्यं मा स्म गमः पार्थ नैतत्त्वय्युपपद्यते।
क्षुद्रं हृदयदौर्बल्यं त्यक्त्वोत्तिष्ठ परंतप॥

Meaning

The English translation and explanation of the Sanskrit shloka:

O Partha (Arjuna), do not yield to unmanliness. It does not become you. Shake off this base faint-heartedness and arise, O scorcher of enemies!

This verse emphasizes the importance of courage and determination in the face of challenges.
